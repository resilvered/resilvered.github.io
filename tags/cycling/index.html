SRAM 48/33 × 10-36 vs Shimano 50/34 × 11-34: The Gear Range Showdown

If you’re building a road bike or debating a groupset upgrade in 2026, two configurations dominate the conversation: SRAM’s 48/33 crankset with a 10-36 cassette and Shimano’s 50/34 with an 11-34. One promises wider range. The other leans on tradition. But which actually delivers where it counts — on the climb, in the sprint, and across every shift in between? I ran the numbers. Here’s what they say.
